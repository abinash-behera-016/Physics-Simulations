Projectile Motion with Horizontal Projection

Below you can input any x co-ordinate value on the projectile and calculate corresponding values of y, vₓ, vᵧ and v. The y co-ordinate value shown is the downward vertical distance covered.

In the case of Horizontal projectile motion, the object is projected horizontally with some initial speed 'u' at a certain height 'h' above the ground. The body moves under the influence of two simultaneous independent motions:
(i) Constant horizontal velocity 'u'
(ii) Vertically downward motion accelerated with constant acceleration 'g'
The general equation of motion is given by, \( y = \frac{g}{2u^2} x^2 \)
Since this is a quadratic function, the trajectory (path) of the projectile is a parabola.
You can play around with different values of the initial speed (u) and height above ground (h) to see the trajectory under different supplied values.
The standard value of 'g' is set to 9.81 m/s². You can put your desired values to simulate the motion on different planets i.e., 3.7 m/s² for Mars, 24.5 m/s² for Jupiter, etc.
